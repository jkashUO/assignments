A Relationship Journey

Told Through the Moments Remembered Most

By: Josh Kashinsky

Ferry Street, Troy, NY

I met my future-wife at a fraternity in upstate New York. Although our first meeting was not at the Ruck, our friendship was built on (being the designated driver) my ability to drive her manual transmission car up Ferry Street Hill after a night out at the Ruck.

National Museum of Natural History, Washington, DC

I proposed at the Lincoln Memorial. The back side of the memorial faces the Potomac river with views to Alexandria and Arlington Cemetary. It was her favorite place in DC and I knew from the moment she told me that I would propose there. But this map shows what I truly remember from that day, which is the moment when I realized that I had lost the family ring that her grandmother gave me. I don't think I've ever been as frightened as I was when I came out of the bathroom and realized the ring was gone. The ring was recovered and all is now well, but this is the part of the day that sticks with me more than anything else.

Maymont Park, Richmond VA

She grew up in Richmond and the first time she took me to Maymont I knew I wanted to get married there. A Gilded-Age estate that was donated to the city of Richmond as a park in 1925, the mansion includes an Japanese garden and an Italian garden with a fountain. We were taking pictures with the wedding photographer before the ceremony and when I sat on the edge of the fountain I dipped the tails of my coat in the water. There were many memorable moments from my wedding, but the photographer's assistant helping me wring out my tuxedo coat while the photographer laughed and took pictures is what sits with me.
